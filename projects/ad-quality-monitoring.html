Ad Quality Monitoring System

Toxic ad creatives — broken close buttons, misleading content, audio bombs — are a silent retention killer. I designed a monitoring system that surfaces bad advertisers from millions of impressions before they damage user trust.

01 — Why this matters

Bad ads cost more than the revenue they generate.

F2P mobile games rely heavily on rewarded video and interstitial ads. The mediation layer hands off creative selection to ad networks — which means the studio doesn't directly control which creative a player sees. Most are fine. A small fraction are toxic: close buttons that don't work, misleading "you won!" claims, audio that blasts at full volume, malware redirects, or simply offensive content.

One bad impression doesn't move a daily KPI. But repeated exposure measurably increases churn, decreases ad opt-in, and corrodes the rewarded-ad economy. The challenge: how do you find the bad advertisers when you have hundreds of thousands of unique creatives a week?

The core insight

Toxic ads leave a behavioral fingerprint. Users skip them earlier, close the app immediately after, watch fewer ads in subsequent sessions, or reduce session count overall. The signal exists — it just needs to be aggregated at the advertiser-creative level, not the user level.

02 — Approach

From raw mediation logs to a watchlist.

The system pipeline runs daily and produces a ranked list of "suspicious advertisers" for the operations team to review and potentially blocklist via mediation.

1
Aggregate impressions by advertiser × creative × placement
Pull raw events from Firebase + mediation platforms. Normalize advertiser identifiers across networks (some report by package, others by creative ID). Filter to advertisers with enough impressions for stable statistics.
2
Compute behavioral health metrics per advertiser
Post-impression session continuation rate, time-to-app-close after impression, subsequent ad opt-in rate, and skip-rate distribution. Each metric has a baseline computed from the entire ad universe.
3
Score against baseline — z-scores & thresholds
An advertiser whose post-impression behavior is multiple standard deviations worse than the network average gets flagged. Single-metric outliers are warnings; multi-metric outliers are blocklist candidates.
4
Surface in a Tableau dashboard with action handles
Operations team sees a sortable table with risk score, sample creatives (where available), and one-click links to the mediation blocklist for the relevant network.

03 — The deliverable

What was shipped.

The system produces a weekly Tableau dashboard plus a ranked HTML report for the operations team. The deliverable includes:

  • A sortable list of the top suspicious advertisers by composite risk score (combining bounce rate, ad-escape rate, post-impression behavior, and revenue contribution).
  • Per-advertiser drill-down: impression count, eCPM, sample creative, post-impression behavioral metrics vs. the ecosystem baseline.
  • Color-coded risk flags (single-metric warnings vs. multi-metric blocklist candidates).
  • Direct links into the relevant ad-mediation platform's blocklist UI for one-click action.
  • Trend tracking: which advertisers are repeat offenders, which were successfully blocked, and the studio-wide trend in ad-quality risk over time.

The actual report contains advertiser-level data and remains the property of my employer, so it isn't published here. I'm happy to walk through the methodology and discuss specific design decisions in an interview setting under appropriate confidentiality.

Why I'm proud of this one

What started as ad-hoc "check on this bad advertiser" requests became a repeatable, automated pipeline. The system now runs without analyst intervention — turning a recurring fire-drill into background hygiene.

04 — What this showcases

Skills demonstrated.

System design

From ad-hoc to monitoring

Took a problem that previously required manual investigation and built a repeatable, automated pipeline that runs without analyst intervention.

Statistical thinking

Behavioral baselines

Single-impression behavior is too noisy. Aggregating to advertiser-level and comparing against ecosystem baselines surfaces real outliers.

Cross-platform data

Mediation normalization

Different ad networks report identifiers differently. The system normalizes across platforms so an advertiser blocked on one network isn't accidentally allowed on another.

Operational impact

Action over insight

The report doesn't just flag bad actors — it provides a one-click path to the blocklist. Insight without an action handle stays insight.

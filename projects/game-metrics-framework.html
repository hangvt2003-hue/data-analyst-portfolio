Game Metrics Framework — From DAU to LTV

01 — Why this exists

Most analytics confusion isn't about the math.

It's about which metric to point at. New analysts and game designers walk into a dashboard with twenty KPIs and no map of how they connect. "Is engagement up?" is the wrong question if engagement and monetization can move in opposite directions on the same day.

I wrote this brief during my training period as a way to compress two months of internal learning into one referenceable document. New team members now use it to onboard themselves without re-asking the same questions about ROAS decomposition, eCPM decay, or why engagement metrics segment by user type.

In one line

Every F2P mobile game lives or dies by ROAS. ROAS = Revenue ÷ Ad Spend. Everything else is just a more useful way to look at one of those two numbers.

02 — Approach

Building the metric tree, top-down.

Rather than listing definitions alphabetically, the document is structured as a causal tree rooted at ROAS — every leaf is a metric a stakeholder might ask about, and every internal node is the relationship that ties leaves together.

1
General metrics tree
ROAS at the root, branching into Revenue (LTV → engagement, retention, ARPU) and Ad Spend (CPI → installs, conversion, ASO/creative quality).
2
Monetization deep-dive
How mediation, ad networks, and waterfall logic actually work — and which metrics surface real problems vs. cosmetic noise. Includes the eCPM decay mental model.
3
User segmentation
Why looking at "average user" hides almost everything. Segments by engagement level, ad-watch behavior, and IAP propensity — each with its own monetization profile.
4
Action handles
For every metric, the document explicitly answers: can the team move it directly, or is it a downstream symptom? This separates "monitor" from "intervene."

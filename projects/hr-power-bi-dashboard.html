HR Power BI Dashboard

An end-to-end workforce analytics dashboard built during my BI internship at KPIM (a Microsoft Partner). Headcount, turnover, tenure, internal mobility, and attrition distribution — designed for HR leadership to act on.

01 — Why this exists

HR data lives in eight places. Decisions need it in one.

HR teams typically operate from a payroll system, a recruiting system, a performance system, and several spreadsheets. Each tool answers one question. None of them connects "what's our attrition rate this quarter" with "which roles are we hiring for" with "which teams have stagnant tenure" — the questions HR leadership actually asks.

The brief: build a single Power BI dashboard that consolidates these sources and surfaces the metrics HR leadership needs to make decisions about hiring, retention, and team structure.

02 — Approach

From source data to dashboard.

1
Data modeling — star schema
Designed a star schema centered on a FactEmployment table. Dimension tables for Employee, Department, Role, and Date. The schema supports time-intelligence queries (period-over-period turnover, tenure-by-cohort) without hairy DAX.
2
Power Query for data wrangling
Source data was inconsistent — date formats varied across systems, role titles weren't standardized, and historical headcount required reconstructing from event logs. Power Query handled the cleaning pipeline, parameterized for reuse.
3
DAX measures — the analytical layer
Wrote calculated measures for: rolling-12-month attrition, voluntary vs. involuntary turnover split, internal mobility rate, time-to-fill by role family, and a tenure-distribution histogram. Heavy use of CALCULATE, FILTER, and time-intelligence functions.
4
Visual design for the audience
HR leadership reads dashboards differently than analysts. Headline KPIs at top. Drill-downs by department on click. Custom org-chart visual using HTML content — see my blog post on this technique. Gantt chart for individual employee history.
